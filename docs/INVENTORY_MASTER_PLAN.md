## Section 1: How the Numbers Were Calculated

### The Two Questions

1. **How much will we sell?** — per channel, per month, May 2026–Jan 2027
2. **How much must be left over in January?** — 90-day buffer so we never hit zero before the next order arrives

### Demand: Use the Maximum, Not the Average

For each channel and each month, we compare 2024 and 2025 actual sales and take the **higher number**. Planning to the max means we're ready for a strong Q4 — if we've ever sold that many in a given month, we need to be ready to do it again.

**Example — Kids Journal, October (global):**

| | Units |
| :--- | ---: |
| 2024 October | 3,247 |
| 2025 October | 2,770 |
| **We plan for** | **3,247** ← the higher of the two |

### Safety Buffer: 90 Days of Stock After January

The buffer is the average of what each channel historically sells in February, March, and April. That amount must still be in the warehouse on February 1st — it's our safety net before the next order cycle completes.

**Example — Kids Journal global buffer:**

| Month | Historical Sales | Average |
| :--- | :--- | ---: |
| Feb | 8,761, 3,682, 2,779 | **5,074** |
| Mar | 8,221, 3,184, 3,916 | **5,107** |
| Apr | 5,733, 4,147 | **4,940** |
| **Total 90-day buffer** | | **15,121** |

### Print vs Transfer Decision

**If a channel is short and we need a new print run:** the print ships direct from the printer to that channel — US FBA, CA FBA, or AU. We do not route through hubs. Hub stock stays in hubs to serve domestic and wholesale orders.

**If global stock is sufficient (no print run):** we reposition hub stock (HBG/SLI → US FBA, CA Hub → CA FBA) to fill channel deficits. No new units are ordered.

**UK transfers (UK → AU for journals, UK → EU) always happen** when those markets have a deficit — regardless of whether we're printing.

| HBG / SLI → US FBA | ✅ If not printing | Skipped when a print run is active |
| CA Hub → CA FBA | ✅ If not printing | Same rule |
| UK → US / CA | ❌ | Not a valid route |
